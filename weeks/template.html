How many pages will you need? In what order should the user navigate those pages?

    1. Select the text or content that you want to present on your website. This could be a story, article, poem, or any other written material. If you don't know what to choose, you can select any of articles or text from readings.design
    2. Break down the text into key sections or chapters
    3. List down the pages you will need based on the key sections identified earlier. Each major section or chapter might correspond to a separate page on your website
    4. Determine the order in which the user should navigate through the pages. Think about the flow of the story or content and how it should unfold for the reader
    5. Explore opportunities for non-linear navigation. Decide if there are interactive elements, branching paths, or links that allow users to explore the content in a non-linear way. This takes advantage of the web's unique capabilities
    6. Use pen and paper to sketch a visual representation of your sitemap. Draw boxes for each page and connect them with arrows to show the flow of navigation. Consider including notes or annotations to explain the purpose of each page
